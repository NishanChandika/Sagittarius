Project Scope

Literature Survey

Chronic Kidney Disease of Unknown Etiology (CKDu) has been a major national health burden in Sri Lanka for three decades, primarily affecting rural farmers in the North Central Province. Studies indicate that the disease's prevalence can range from 2% to 15%, with approximately 400,000 cases and over 20,000 fatalities [1][2].


The geographical distribution of CKDu cases correlates strongly with areas facing water quality issues. Researchers have proposed various water-related factors as potential triggers for CKDu, including the presence of heavy metals, agricultural chemicals, and other contaminants in water sources [3]. However, the direct causal link between specific water quality parameters and the high incidence of CKDu remains a subject of ongoing investigation [1][3].


The World Health Organization has emphasized the importance of access to clean water in affected regions as a preventive measure. However, effective monitoring and assessment of water quality in rural areas are severely hampered by the lack of technical infrastructure and other technological obstacles, leading to ineffective and unproductive localized assessments [1][10].


Recent studies have explored innovative approaches to water quality monitoring. For instance, Toh Yin Wei et al. developed an automated water quality monitoring and forecasting system for aquaculture, incorporating AI-based algorithms, wireless sensor networks, and real-time monitoring of parameters such as temperature, pH, and dissolved oxygen [4]. Similarly, Zulhani Rasin and Mohd Rizal Abdullah proposed a Zigbee-based wireless sensor network for flexible and efficient water quality monitoring [5].


In the realm of autonomous monitoring systems, Jorge F. et al. designed an Autonomous Surface Vehicle named Squirtle for inland water environmental surveillance, featuring GPS accuracy, inertial sensors, and obstacle detection capabilities [7]. These advancements in technology and approach provide valuable insights for developing more effective water quality monitoring solutions in rural Sri Lankan contexts.


The literature also highlights the potential of machine learning in improving water quality prediction and classification. Mahmoud et al. demonstrated the viability of various machine learning models, with Gradient Boosting achieving a classification accuracy of 99.5% for water quality assessment [8].


This comprehensive review of existing research underscores the critical need for innovative, autonomous, and cost-effective solutions for continuous water quality monitoring in rural reservoirs of Sri Lanka, particularly in regions affected by CKDu.

Research Gap

Lack of Real-Time Monitoring: Current water quality monitoring methods in Sri Lanka's rural reservoirs are predominantly manual and periodic, failing to provide real-time data crucial for timely interventions. This gap leaves authorities and communities without up-to-date information on water quality, hindering rapid response to emerging issues.

Limited Geographical Coverage: Existing monitoring systems are unable to cover vast areas of water bodies effectively, leaving significant portions of reservoirs unmonitored and potentially at risk. This limitation results in incomplete data sets and potential blind spots in water quality assessment across rural areas.

Technological Limitations: There is a notable absence of advanced, automated systems capable of continuous monitoring in remote areas with limited infrastructure and connectivity. This technological gap impedes the deployment of sophisticated monitoring solutions in the regions that need them most.

Lack of Predictive Analytics: Current methods do not incorporate predictive modeling or machine learning techniques to forecast potential water quality issues before they become critical. This absence of predictive capabilities limits proactive management and prevention strategies for water-related health risks.

Power and Sustainability Issues: Existing solutions often lack sustainable power sources, limiting their ability to operate continuously in remote locations without frequent maintenance. This gap in sustainable power solutions hinders long-term, uninterrupted monitoring efforts in rural areas.

Inadequate Early Warning Systems: There is a significant gap in systems capable of providing timely alerts about deteriorating water quality, crucial for preventing health crises like CKDu outbreaks. The lack of effective early warning mechanisms leaves communities vulnerable to water quality degradation and its health impacts.

Research Problem & Solution

The Challenge

3.6 million communities in Sri Lanka affected by severe water quality issues.

The North Central Province of Sri Lanka faces a critical health crisis with the high prevalence of Chronic Kidney Disease of Unknown Etiology (CKDu). This disease, primarily affecting rural farmers, has seen a dramatic increase, with prevalence rates ranging from 2% to 15%.

Approximately 400,000 cases have been reported, resulting in over 20,000 fatalities. The root cause is believed to be linked to water quality, exacerbated by chemical pollution from agricultural activities and inadequate monitoring systems.

Our Solution: Sagittarius

An autonomous floating robot for continuous water quality monitoring.

Sagittarius is designed to address the critical need for real-time, comprehensive water quality monitoring in rural reservoirs. This innovative solution combines advanced technology with sustainable design to provide continuous, accurate data on water quality parameters.

Key features include solar power for 24/7 operation, LoRaWAN technology for long-range data transmission, and integrated deep learning for predictive analytics. The robot's ability to generate geo-tagged data and water quality heat maps offers unprecedented insights for decision-makers.

Expected Impact

By providing reliable, scalable, and long-term water quality monitoring, Sagittarius aims to:

  1. Improve public health outcomes in affected regions
  2. Enable informed decision-making by authorities and stakeholders
  3. Establish early warning systems for water quality degradation
  4. Contribute to sustainable water resource management in rural areas

Research Objectives

Multi-Sensor Integration

Develop a sophisticated multi-sensor device capable of accurately measuring critical water quality parameters, including pH, turbidity, dissolved oxygen (DO), total dissolved solids (TDS), and humidity.

Real-Time Analytics

Implement a system for real-time analytical data processing and predictive monitoring, utilizing advanced algorithms to detect even slight variations in water quality.

Autonomous Navigation

Design and implement self-navigation capabilities with obstacle avoidance, enabling the robot to efficiently cover large reservoir areas independently.

Long-Range Communication

Integrate LoRaWAN technology for efficient, long-range, low-power data transmission, ensuring reliable communication in remote rural areas with limited infrastructure.

Sustainable Operation

Develop a power system combining solar charging with efficient DC battery usage to enable continuous 24/7 operation in remote locations.

Intelligent Analysis

Implement machine learning models for water quality prediction and classification, aiming for high accuracy in assessing water safety and detecting potential sensor defects.

Ultimate Goal

Create a novel, comprehensive approach for monitoring water resources in rural Sri Lankan reservoirs, addressing the critical need for effective water quality assessment to combat health issues like CKDu and improve overall public health outcomes.

Methodology

The Sagittarius robot employs a comprehensive approach to water quality monitoring, integrating various technologies and components for efficient and accurate data collection and analysis.

Key Features

Autonomous Navigation

Utilizes sonar sensors and a drone motor for efficient obstacle avoidance and coverage of large reservoir areas.

LoRaWAN Communication

Implements long-range, low-power data transmission for real-time updates in remote locations.

Sustainable Power

Integrates solar panels with a DC battery system for continuous 24/7 operation.

GPS Tracking

Provides accurate location monitoring and enhances security measures against theft or unauthorized access.

Multi-Parameter Sensing

Measures key water quality parameters including pH, Turbidity, Dissolved Oxygen (DO), Total Dissolved Solids (TDS), and Humidity.

Advanced Security

Incorporates PIR and ultrasonic sensors for detecting unauthorized interference or attempted theft.

Data Flow Process

  1. Sensors collect water quality data and environmental information.
  2. ESP32 MCU processes the collected data.
  3. LoRa sender transmits data to the LoRa receiver.
  4. Gateway relays data to the Firebase database via internet connection.
  5. Water Quality Prediction module analyzes data using machine learning models.
  6. Results are displayed on a heat map and sent as alerts to administrators and users.

Technologies Used

Sagittarius integrates cutting-edge technologies to ensure efficient water quality monitoring and data analysis:

Python

Used for data processing, analysis, and implementing machine learning models for water quality prediction.

React

Powers the web interface for real-time data visualization and user interaction with the Sagittarius system.

TensorFlow

Implements deep learning models for advanced water quality analysis and prediction.

MediaPipe

Facilitates computer vision tasks for visual inspection and analysis of water samples.

Whisper

Integrated for potential voice command features, enhancing user interaction with the robot.

Firebase

Provides real-time database functionality and user authentication for secure data management.

AYLIEN

Utilized for text analysis and natural language processing of water quality reports.

LoRaWAN

Enables long-range, low-power communication for data transmission from remote locations.
